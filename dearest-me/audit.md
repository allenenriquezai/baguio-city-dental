## 1. No owned website — Facebook is not a website

**Finding:** When you search "Dearest Me Wellness Spa Baguio" online, there is no clinic website. Your Facebook page at facebook.com/dearestmewelness is your only web presence. New clients who aren't already on Facebook, or who search on Google, find nothing they can book from.

**Why this matters:**
- Clients searching for "massage Baguio City" or "wellness spa near me" land on competitors who have real websites with booking buttons.
- You can't run targeted Facebook or Google Ads to a Facebook page alone — you need a landing page to track conversions.
- Your 73 Google reviews are an asset. Without a website, you can't turn that Google traffic into direct bookings.
- A website also lets you show your prices, your team, your vibe — the things that make a first-time client say yes before they even walk in.

**Competitor examples:**
- Baguio wellness spas with booking pages capture walk-in traffic online before it becomes walk-in traffic.
- The rule: clients who can book in 2 clicks book. Clients who have to send a DM and wait often go elsewhere.

**Fix:** A simple website with your services, rates, and a "Book Now" button linked to your Facebook Messenger or a free booking tool like Calendly or Setmore.

**Screenshot:** None

## 2. Rating at 3.9★ — just below the trust floor

**Finding:** Your Google Business Profile shows 3.9★ across 73 reviews. For a wellness and massage spa, the trust floor most new clients look for is 4.0★ or above. You're one point below it.

**Why this matters:**
- A new client choosing between two spas will almost always pick the one with 4.0+ over the one with 3.9.
- 73 reviews is a real base — this is fixable. You don't need hundreds of new reviews. You need to get your score above 4.0 and keep it there.
- Negative reviews that went without a public response signal to new clients that the business doesn't care. Responding to reviews (positive and negative) raises perceived quality.

**Competitor examples:**
- Baguio spas with 4.5+ ratings and 100+ reviews dominate the top of Google search results.
- A consistent review-request message sent after each session ("Hi! Hope you enjoyed your session — would you leave us a Google review? [link]") gets a 15–30% response rate in most PH wellness businesses.

**Fix:** Two steps: (1) respond to all existing reviews, especially the negative ones — professionally, briefly, kindly. (2) Send a short follow-up message to every client after their session asking for a Google review with a direct link.

**Screenshot:** None

## 3. No online booking visible

**Finding:** Clients who want to book at Dearest Me Wellness Spa must send a DM on Facebook and wait for a manual reply. There is no booking button, no calendar link, and no instant confirmation visible on your page or Google listing.

**Why this matters:**
- Every minute a new client waits for a reply is a minute they might book someone else.
- Late-night impulse bookings (common for Baguio visitors on weekend trips) are lost entirely if no one is online to reply.
- Manual DM handling is also a staff time drain — your front desk handles inquiries that a system could handle automatically.

**Competitor examples:**
- Spas with Setmore or similar free booking tools show a "Book Now" button directly on their Google Business Profile and Facebook page.
- Setmore is free for solo operators and small teams, takes 30 minutes to set up, and sends automatic appointment confirmations and reminders.

**Fix:** Set up a free booking page (Setmore or Calendly) and link it on your Facebook page and Google Business Profile. Clients book themselves, get a confirmation, and your team sees the calendar update in real time.

**Screenshot:** None

## 4. No auto-reply or response-time signal on Facebook

**Finding:** Your Facebook page does not show a response time badge (e.g. "Typically replies within an hour"). This means there is no system handling first contact when you or your staff are unavailable.

**Why this matters:**
- Facebook rewards pages that respond quickly with a "Very responsive" badge — which shows to new visitors and builds trust before they even send a message.
- Without an auto-reply, a potential client who messages at 10pm gets silence until morning. By morning, they've already booked elsewhere.
- An instant auto-reply that says "Hi! Thanks for reaching out. We'll confirm your booking within the hour. In the meantime, here are our services and rates: [link]" buys you time AND makes a great first impression.

**Competitor examples:**
- Businesses with Facebook auto-reply set up maintain "Very responsive" badges and reduce missed leads by 30–50%.

**Fix:** Set up a Facebook auto-reply in your Page settings (it's built-in and free). The message should acknowledge the inquiry, set a response expectation, and include a link to your services or booking page.
